# Wang Yangming: Awakening in Longchang

**Ming Dynasty · 1472-1529** | Founder of the School of Mind, originator of "Unity of Knowledge and Action"

---

## Young Wang Yangming: A Life of Five Passions

In his youth, Wang Yangming was consumed by **five passions**:

- **Passion for Chivalry** - He wanted to be a knight-errant, practicing swordsmanship and martial arts
- **Passion for Archery** - He wanted to be a general, studying military strategy
- **Passion for Literature** - He wanted to be a scholar, devouring poetry and prose
- **Passion for Immortality** - He wanted to achieve immortality, studying Daoism
- **Passion for Buddhism** - He wanted to achieve enlightenment, studying Zen Buddhism

He tried everything. He wanted to become everything. This was not being lost—it was **searching**.

> *"He eventually discovered: external pursuits are not the answer. The answer lies within the heart."*

---

## Awakening in Longchang: Finding Truth in the Lowest Valley

At 37, Wang Yangming offended a powerful official and was exiled to Longchang, Guizhou province.

What was Longchang?

> A barren frontier. Venomous insects everywhere. No one spoke his language. No shelter.  
> He lived in a stone cave, chopping wood, boiling water, gathering herbs.  
> When his servants fell ill one by one, he cared for them.

This was the lowest point of his life. But it was precisely here that he **awakened**.

One night, he suddenly shouted:

> **"The way of the sage is complete within my own nature!"**  
> The truth of the sage is already in my heart! No need to seek it outside!

This is **"Mind is Principle"**: truth is not in books, not in teachers—it is within you.

---

## Unity of Knowledge and Action: Not Theory, But Practice

After his awakening, Wang Yangming proposed the **Unity of Knowledge and Action**:

> *"Knowledge is the beginning of action. Action is the completion of knowledge."*  
> *"There has never been knowledge without action. Those who know but do not act simply do not know."*

This is not about "if you know it, you should do it." It means **true knowledge is inseparable from action**.

If you claim to know filial piety but never act filially toward your parents, you do not truly know what filial piety is.

Knowledge and action are **the same thing**.

---

## Extending Innate Knowledge: The Core of Mind Philosophy

In his later years, Wang Yangming proposed **"Extending Innate Knowledge"**:

> *"Innate knowledge"* — the moral discernment inherent in every human heart  
> *"Extend"* — apply this innate knowledge to everything you do  
> *"Everyone has Confucius within"* — every person carries a sage inside them

You don't need to study morality from the outside. Your heart **already knows** what is good and what is evil.

You only need to **extend** this innate knowledge into everything you do.

---

## Core Growth Lessons

- **The Longchang Principle** — The lowest valley is often the gateway to awakening
- **Mind is Principle** — Truth is not outside you; it is within
- **Unity of Knowledge and Action** — True knowledge is inseparable from action
- **Extending Innate Knowledge** — Your heart already knows right from wrong; just extend it
